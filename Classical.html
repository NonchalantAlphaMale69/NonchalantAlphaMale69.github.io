Classical Music

Classical music is a broad, umbrella term referring to the sophisticated musical traditions of the Western world, generally dating from the medieval period to the present day. Its history is typically divided into distinct erasincluding Baroque, Classical, and Romanticeach characterized by evolving forms like the symphony and concerto, and the foundational development of Western musical notation. Originating primarily in European liturgical and courtly contexts, classical music has always striven for emotional depth, structural complexity, and intellectual rigor. Today, it remains highly significant as the cornerstone of Western art music, influencing modern film scores, providing educational frameworks for musical study, and continuing to be performed and appreciated in concert halls worldwide for its enduring beauty and profound expressive power.
