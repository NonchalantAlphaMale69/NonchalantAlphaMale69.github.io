K-Pop

The modern K-pop genre originated in South Korea in 1992 with the debut of Seo Taiji and Boys, who revolutionized the local music scene by blending Western pop, hip-hop, R&B, and electronic dance music with Korean lyrics and dynamic choreography. This innovative approach, which broke away from traditional ballads, sparked the creation of the highly structured "idol system" and entertainment agencies (like SM, YG, and JYP) dedicated to training artists for performance and visual appeal. The subsequent "Korean Wave" (Hallyu) in the late 1990s and early 2000s, propelled by digital platforms and social media, helped K-pop gain traction across Asia and eventually the world.
